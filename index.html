  1. 01

    My main business has never changed. For six years I've stayed deeply focused on the real-economy side of renewable energy and carbon trading. Going forward, that work will lean further into the intersection of AI and industry.

  2. 02

In the three days starting April 1, I built three commercially viable AI products on DeepSeek V2 — for renewable energy, carbon sinks, and electricity trading — burning more than 400 million tokens. We've since brought in one of the country's top cube coaches to train the team for the national competition — and we keep polishing the cube-learning web app along the way.

CO-FOUNDER

R.W is the younger brother of B.W, currently studying in the United States.

Drawing on his prior experience building with AI, he designed and built RBW alongside his sister, with a single shared goal: to bring the traditional Chinese casting arts — Xiao Liu Ren, Ziwei Doushu, and the lineages around them — to a wider audience without diluting them.

He handles the technology and the look of the studio's online presence.

Living and studying in the West, he is the half of RBW that meets these traditions from the other side — translating, framing, and building so they reach an audience that grew up in a different language and a different century.

CO-FOUNDER
B.W

B.W is the older sister of R.W, with whom she co-founded RBW.

She spent eight years abroad before returning to build her career in renewable energy and the carbon markets, and she brings that same long-horizon, systems-minded approach to the studio — patient, structured, and concerned with what actually lasts.

Outside her career, the traditional Chinese readings have always been the quiet through-line of her life — a subject she has read about, asked questions about, and returned to since she was young.

Within RBW, she is the half that holds the Chinese end of the table: the texts, the lineages, and the language these arts were first written in.

Together, the two of them are how East and West actually sit at the same table.
